# Closest competitors on Free AP Practice's path to 1M users

_Research date: 2026-07-09. This excludes the large incumbent publishers and College Board. All product, pricing, and traction claims below come from the competitor's current first-party pages; figures are therefore **self-reported** unless stated otherwise._

## Executive take

The meaningful competitive set is not one company: it is six focused products that can each own one part of the AP student journey. The biggest near-term risk is **a better first-session outcome**—a realistic timed exam, a trusted question bank, or rubric feedback—not a giant company’s brand.

| Priority | Competitor | What it can win | Public traction evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [AcornPrep](https://www.acornprep.com/) | Sign-up-gated all-in-one practice with a paid full-access tier, plus a strong expert-written quality claim | Explicitly says it is brand new and does not have thousands of students. |
| 2 | [AP Scholr](https://apscholr.com/) | Sign-up-gated all-AP suite: notes, practice, full exams, tutor, and writing feedback, with ads | No public user or school count found. |
| 3 | [EazyAP](https://www.eazyap.com/) | Full-length timed simulations and a large SEO surface | No public user or school count found. |
| 4 | [AGrader](https://agrader.ai/) | Rubric-based writing feedback, with the clearest public usage signal | Claims 10,000+ essays graded. |
| 5 | [FRQ Review](https://www.frqreview.com/) | No-account, unlimited, photo-to-feedback FRQ grading | No public usage count found. |
| 6 | [Calcify AI](https://calcifyai.app/) | A polished, mobile, single-subject depth play for AP Calculus AB | No public download/user count found. |

Free AP Practice's public baseline is 20+ subjects, unlimited no-signup AI MCQs, instant explanations, and an optional account for history and insights ([homepage](https://freeappractice.org/)). That means the defensible wedge is **the fastest reliable practice start across many APs**—not being the broadest study suite.

## 1. AcornPrep — closest all-in-one free competitor

**Offer and access.** AcornPrep says it is built by AP students and is “premium AP prep, free for every student.” It lists 10 courses, 4,000+ questions (400+ per course), unlimited MCQ practice with explanations, AI rubric grading for interactive FRQs, an AI tutor, and progress/streak tracking. It also promotes flashcards, podcasts, guides, worked examples, mind maps, and analytics. [Source: AcornPrep homepage](https://www.acornprep.com/).

**Pricing/traction.** Its marketing page says “100% free, forever.” However, your July 2026 product audit found that practice requires an account and that a paid tier gates full access. Treat the live, in-product access model—not the headline—as the decision-relevant comparison. Its site also says it is brand new and does not yet have thousands of students—important because this is a close product threat, not yet a proven distribution threat.

**Implementation signal (your audit).** It appears to be Claude-vibe-coded. This is not independently verified, so it should not be used as an external-facing claim. Internally, it is useful only as a reminder that a broad-looking feature set is not proof of durable quality, reliability, or distribution.

**Threat.** Its “every question expert-written / expert verified” language is a direct trust counter-position to AI-generated questions. Its FRQ loop makes the product feel complete.

**Opportunity.** Do not match the feature catalog. Make Free AP Practice’s quality legible: explain alignment, give excellent explanations, make reporting/fixing visible, and go deeper in the highest-demand topics. Keep the first question faster than an all-in-one onboarding experience.

## 2. AP Scholr — closest free AI-suite competitor

**Offer and access.** AP Scholr calls itself “built by a student, for students” and “free forever.” It says it covers all 39 AP courses with unit-aware AI notes, flashcards, a tutor, timed full exams with predicted 1–5 scores, and AI grading for FRQs/DBQs/SAQs/LEQs. It claims notes are scoped to the 2026 College Board CED and feedback to the current rubric. [Source: AP Scholr homepage](https://apscholr.com/).

**Pricing/traction.** It advertises $0 forever, no card, no trial, and no upsell. Your July 2026 product audit found that practice requires an account and that the product displays ads. That means its genuine comparison is not “free with no strings,” but a sign-up-and-ad-supported experience. No first-party audience count was found.

**Implementation signal (your audit).** It appears fully vibe-coded. This is an internal observation, not independently verified evidence about its codebase. The strategic implication is the same: do not mistake surface-area parity for a lasting advantage.

**Threat.** Its promise is “everything a student opens during the two weeks before an AP exam.” A student may perceive a full learning system where Free AP Practice currently feels like one strong tool.

**Opportunity.** Own a crisp alternative: a student can enter from search, choose an exact class/unit/topic, start immediately, and get a trustworthy explanation. Resist a race to add 39-course notes. Focus on dependable question quality and returning learners’ weak-area practice.

## 3. EazyAP — exam-simulation and SEO threat

**Offer and access.** EazyAP offers full-length timed simulations intended to mirror exam sections, question types, and structure, then gives estimated 1–5 scores and personalized FRQ feedback. It lists 21 AP subjects and has course-specific test pages, diagnostics, score calculators, FRQ guides, and blog posts. [Source: EazyAP homepage](https://www.eazyap.com/).

**Pricing.** The first simulation is free; it sells exam credits rather than a subscription: 2 for $16, 5 for $34, 10 for $57, and 30 teacher exams for $139. [Source: EazyAP homepage](https://www.eazyap.com/).

**Traction.** No first-party user count was found.

**Threat.** It competes on an outcome students understand instantly: “practice like the real exam.” Its many AP-specific guide, diagnostic, and calculator pages create a search-distribution machine.

**Opportunity.** Expand only the pages that lead directly into practice: every course/unit/topic page should solve a search intent and let the student practice that topic with one click. A lightweight timed-set or exam-mode loop can meet the core need without reproducing every simulation format.

## 4. AGrader — strongest FRQ-feedback and verified-usage signal

**Offer and access.** AGrader offers free practice tests, MCQs, and AI grading for essays across 9 AP subjects (plus IB English). It accepts uploaded/pasted LEQs, DBQs, SAQs, and other AP/IB essays, gives rubric-aligned feedback in about 60 seconds, and tracks rubric-area progress. [Source: AGrader homepage](https://agrader.ai/).

**Pricing.** Free includes three lifetime essays, 35 lifetime MCQs, and a basic score breakdown. The displayed Pro price is $5/month with monthly/yearly billing options; it includes 20 essays/month or 100/year, 250 MCQs/month or 550/year, simulations, tutor access, and detailed reports. The teacher offering is contact-sales. [Source: AGrader homepage](https://agrader.ai/).

**Traction.** It claims **10,000+ essays graded**. This is the strongest concrete first-party usage signal among this shortlist, but it is still a company claim, not audited analytics.

**Threat.** It owns the moment after a student writes: specific rubric feedback and trend tracking. That is a valuable reason to create an account and pay.

**Opportunity.** Free AP Practice should first make MCQ feedback and weak-topic follow-up unusually useful. If adding writing, begin with one highly-used format/course and test grading consistency against official released samples before promising broad “AP reader” equivalence.

## 5. FRQ Review — the simplest no-signup writing substitute

**Offer and access.** FRQ Review is a student-built AI FRQ grader. A student pastes a prompt, supplies or generates a rubric, uploads a photo or typed response, and receives point-level feedback in roughly 15 seconds. It says it supports every AP subject with FRQs and is not affiliated with College Board. [Source: FRQ Review homepage](https://www.frqreview.com/).

**Pricing.** Basic is free forever, ad-supported, and allows unlimited FRQs without an account. Plus is $3.99/month for no ads, faster grading, and unlimited saved history. [Source: FRQ Review homepage](https://www.frqreview.com/).

**Traction.** No first-party usage figure found.

**Threat.** It has Free AP Practice’s strongest current advantage—no signup—applied to writing. The upload-a-photo interaction also fits how students actually complete FRQs on paper.

**Opportunity.** Protect the no-account habit. A future FRQ feature must preserve it, and it should be explicit that feedback is tied to the rubric supplied rather than presenting AI grading as unquestionable authority.

## 6. Calcify AI — narrow mobile-depth threat

**Offer and access.** Calcify AI focuses exclusively on AP Calculus AB. It offers 1,000+ AP-style questions across all eight units, timed 45-question practice exams with score prediction, unit analytics, native visual diagrams (including slope fields and Riemann sums), AI explanations, offline quizzes, and reminders. [Source: Calcify AI homepage](https://calcifyai.app/).

**Pricing/traction.** It is an App Store product with a 7-day no-card trial, then $6.99/month. No public download or user count was found.

**Threat.** It demonstrates the quality bar that a narrow product can reach: native diagrams, mobile habit loops, and strong subject specificity. A broad 20-subject app can lose a Calc AB student even while offering that subject.

**Opportunity.** Choose one or two strategically important APs and make their question experience excellent—especially visual/stimulus questions—while retaining the broad free catalog. The goal is targeted depth, not a separate app for every course.

## What to do next

1. **Measure and perfect time-to-first-good-question.** This is the shared weak point of broad suites and the clearest Free AP Practice advantage.
2. **Make question trust visible.** Show course/unit/topic alignment and add a report/fix loop. AcornPrep’s “expert written” message proves this is a conversion lever.
3. **Turn SEO into practice, not just reading.** Follow EazyAP’s coverage model only where each page immediately starts relevant practice.
4. **Build depth selectively.** Add realistic timed/stimulus experiences for the APs that drive acquisition; do not start with a generic all-subject FRQ grader.
5. **Keep the account as a reward, not a gate.** After the student gets value, offer history, weak-area review, streaks, and a recommended next question.

## Research limits

- No third-party traffic, downloads, or social claims were used.
- The AcornPrep/AP Scholr sign-up, paid-access, advertising, and implementation observations are from your July 2026 product audit. They are deliberately labeled as such rather than presented as independently verified external facts.
- Product pages can change rapidly. “No public figure found” means none was found on the reviewed first-party page, not that the product has no users.
- The ranking measures likelihood of capturing the same early, search-driven AP student, not company revenue or total market share.
